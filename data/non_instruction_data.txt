Password Policy: All employee passwords must be at least 14 characters long and contain a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special symbols. Passwords must be updated every 90 days. Sharing passwords via chat, email, or oral communication is strictly prohibited. If you suspect your credentials have been compromised, immediately notify the Security Operations Center (SOC).

VPN Access and Security: Remote employees must connect to the corporate network using the Cisco AnyConnect Secure Mobility Client. Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) via Microsoft Authenticator is required for all VPN connections. VPN access is automatically terminated after 8 hours of inactivity. Split tunneling is disabled, meaning all internet traffic is routed through the secure corporate network when connected.

Hardware Procurement Policy: Laptop replacements are scheduled every three years. Standard issue laptops are Lenovo ThinkPad T14 or Apple MacBook Pro 14-inch depending on the department. Requests for non-standard hardware, additional RAM, or external monitors must be approved by the department manager and submitted via the IT Procurement Form on the service portal.

Software Installation Guidelines: Employees are not allowed to install unauthorized third-party software on company-issued devices. All required applications must be installed via the Software Center (Windows) or Self Service (macOS). For software not listed in the standard catalog, employees must submit a Software Request Ticket containing a business justification and licensing details.

Data Backup Procedure: All business-critical documents must be saved to OneDrive for Business or the corporate SharePoint environment. Local drive backups (C:\ drive) are not performed automatically by the IT department. Lost data on local drives due to hardware failure is the responsibility of the employee if not synced to OneDrive.

Phishing and Security Alerts: Phishing attempts should be reported immediately using the 'Report Phishing' button in Outlook. Do not click on links, download attachments, or reply to suspicious emails. The IT security team runs monthly simulations to train staff. If you enter your credentials on a suspected phishing site, change your password immediately and contact the Helpdesk.

Visitor Wireless Network: The guest Wi-Fi network (Company-Guest) is intended for visitors and partners. It does not provide access to internal corporate systems, databases, or local printers. Guest credentials expire after 24 hours. Bandwidth on the guest network is throttled to ensure business operations on the primary network are not impacted.

Workstation Security Lock: Workstations must be locked whenever an employee leaves their desk, even for a short duration. The default screen lock timeout is set to 5 minutes of inactivity. Press Windows Key + L on Windows or Control + Command + Q on macOS to lock your screen manually. Unlocked workstations are subject to audit and disciplinary action.

Incident Management and SLAs: The IT Helpdesk categorizes incidents based on urgency and impact. Priority 1 (P1) incidents are critical business outages and have a resolution SLA of 2 hours. Priority 2 (P2) are high-impact issues affecting multiple users and have an SLA of 4 hours. Priority 3 (P3) are standard issues with an SLA of 24 hours, and Priority 4 (P4) are minor requests resolved within 3 business days.

Remote Desktop Support: IT Support uses Microsoft Teams or TeamViewer for remote assistance. The technician will request permission before initiating a screen-sharing session. The employee must remain at the computer during the session and can terminate the connection at any time. Sensitive personal files should be closed before sharing screens.

Bring Your Own Device (BYOD) Policy: Employees may access corporate email and calendar on personal mobile devices (iOS or Android) provided they install the Mobile Device Management (MDM) profile. The MDM enforces screen locks, device encryption, and allows remote wiping of corporate data in case of device loss. Personal files are not monitored or accessed.

Asset Disposal and Return: Upon termination of employment, all company-owned assets (laptops, monitors, mobile phones, security keys) must be returned to the IT department within 5 business days. Laptops must not be wiped by the employee; the IT team will securely erase all storage drives in compliance with data privacy standards before recycling.

Local Administrator Rights: By default, standard employee user accounts do not have local administrator privileges. Temporary admin rights can be requested through the 'Make Me Admin' application for tasks such as developer setups or driver installations. This access is granted for a maximum duration of 2 hours per request.

Printer and Copier Usage: Shared network printers are configured via the Print Server. To add a printer, search for the printer name in the directory or map it using the UNC path \\printserver\printer_name. Double-sided and black-and-white printing are set as default to conserve paper and ink. Color printing requires approval.

Video Conferencing Equipment: Conference rooms are equipped with Teams Rooms systems. Do not unplug cables, change settings, or modify the physical setup of the equipment. If a system is malfunctioning, report the room number and issue to IT Support immediately. For urgent assistance during a meeting, call the hot-line extension 4444.

Shared Folder Permissions: Access to shared department drives (S:\ drive) is controlled via Active Directory groups. To request access to a folder, the folder owner or department head must submit an Access Request Ticket. Permissions are reviewed quarterly to ensure compliance with the principle of least privilege.

Email Retention and Archiving: Corporate email boxes are capped at 50 GB. Emails older than two years are automatically archived to the Online Archive mailbox, which has unlimited storage. Deleted items are kept in the recovery bin for 30 days before permanent deletion. Legal holds will prevent archiving and deletion when active.

Desktop Monitor Calibration: Standard office monitors are calibrated to sRGB color space. If your role requires high color accuracy (e.g., graphic designers), you may request a professional hardware calibrator. Do not adjust monitor color settings manually without consulting the creative team lead.

IT Equipment Cleaning Guidelines: Laptops, keyboards, and screens should only be cleaned using approved electronics wipes or micro-fiber cloths damp with 70% isopropyl alcohol. Spraying liquid cleaners directly onto screens or keyboards is prohibited as it may seep into the hardware and cause short circuits.

Office Ergononics Setup: IT and HR provide ergonomic assessments for workstations. Employees can request adjustable keyboard trays, vertical mice, ergonomic chairs, or standing desks. Requests require an evaluation by the safety officer and approval from the department manager.

Home Office Internet Reimbursement: Remote workers are eligible for internet connectivity reimbursement up to a standard limit. To qualify, employees must submit their monthly internet bill through the Expense portal. The connection must meet minimum bandwidth requirements of 50 Mbps download and 10 Mbps upload.

Offboarding Account Deactivation: On an employee's last day, their Active Directory account is disabled at 6:00 PM local time. The mailbox is converted to a shared mailbox, and access is delegated to the line manager for 30 days to retrieve business-related communications. After 90 days, the account is deleted.

Onboarding IT Setup: New hires receive their hardware and credentials on their first day during the IT Orientation. The orientation covers setting up MFA, configuring email, connecting to corporate Wi-Fi, and basic security training. HR must submit onboarding requests at least 7 days in advance.

Mobile Phone Allowance: Eligible roles (sales, field engineering, management) receive a company-sponsored mobile phone or a monthly stipend for using their personal phone. International roaming must be approved prior to travel to avoid excessive charges. Personal use should be kept to a minimum.

Security Patch Management: Security patches for operating systems (Windows and macOS) are pushed every Tuesday night. Devices will prompt the user to restart if a reboot is required. Users can delay the restart for up to 3 days, after which the device will automatically restart to apply critical updates.

Hardware Upgrade Request: RAM or SSD upgrades can be requested for development machines if performance is bottlenecked. The request must include a screenshot of Task Manager or Activity Monitor showing high resource utilization (above 85% consistently) during standard work tasks.

System Outages Communication: In the event of a major IT system outage, updates will be posted on the IT Status Page (status.company.com) and broadcast via SMS if email is affected. Do not call the helpdesk for known global outages to keep lines free for individual emergency support.

License Management Policy: Software licenses (e.g., Adobe Creative Cloud, JetBrains, Microsoft 365) are managed centrally. Unused licenses are audited monthly and automatically reclaimed if the software has not been launched for 45 days. Reclaimed licenses can be re-requested if needed again.

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Reset: If you lose or replace your mobile phone, your MFA settings must be reset. To verify your identity, you must contact the Helpdesk via video call or present a government-issued photo ID. Once verified, a temporary bypass code is issued to register the new device.

Data Privacy and GDPR Compliance: All databases containing Personally Identifiable Information (PII) must be encrypted at rest and in transit. Access to PII is restricted to authorized personnel and is logged for auditing. Any suspected data breach must be reported to the Data Protection Officer (DPO) within 24 hours.
